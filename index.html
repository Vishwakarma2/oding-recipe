KITCHEN RECIPES

Trying to cook something in the kitchen might seem like *recipe* for disaster but cooking is a very relaxing and easy task espeically if you follow a well structured recipe.
Here is a list of some east to make recipes.

  • Tea recipe

    • Ingredients:
      • 1 tea bag (black, green, or herbal)
      • 1 cup of hot water
      • 1-2 tsp sugar (optional)
      • Milk (optional)
    • Steps:
      • Boil water in a kettle or on the stove.
      • Place the tea bag in a cup.
      • Pour hot water into the cup over the tea bag.
      • Let it steep for 3-5 minutes (depending on desired strength).
      • Remove the tea bag, and add sugar or milk if desired.
      • Stir and enjoy!

  • Curry

    • Ingredients:
      • 500g chicken (or vegetables for a vegetarian option)
      • 2 tbsp curry powder
      • 1 onion, chopped
      • 2 cloves garlic, minced
      • 1 can of coconut milk (400ml)
      • 1 tbsp olive oil
      • Salt and pepper to taste
      • Rice to serve
    • Steps:
      • Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat.
      • Add chopped onion and minced garlic. Cook until softened.
      • Add chicken (or vegetables) and brown them for about 5-7 minutes.
      • Stir in curry powder, salt, and pepper.
      • Pour in coconut milk, bring to a simmer, and cook for 15-20 minutes.
      • Serve the curry over rice and enjoy!

  • Banana Cake

    • Ingredients:
      • 3 ripe bananas
      • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
      • 1 tsp baking soda
      • ½ tsp salt
      • 1/2 cup butter, softened
      • 1 cup sugar
      • 2 large eggs
      • 1 tsp vanilla extract
      • 1/4 cup buttermilk
    • Steps:
      • Preheat the oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a cake pan.
      • In a bowl, mash the bananas until smooth.
      • In another bowl, mix flour, baking soda, and salt.
      • In a separate bowl, beat together the butter and sugar until creamy.
      • Add the eggs one at a time, then mix in the vanilla extract and mashed bananas.
      • Alternate adding the dry ingredients and buttermilk, starting and ending with the dry ingredients.
      • Pour the batter into the prepared cake pan and bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
      • Let the cake cool, then frost with your favorite icing or serve plain.
